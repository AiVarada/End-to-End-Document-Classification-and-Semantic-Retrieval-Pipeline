QUESTION ABOUT MEDICATION INTERACTION — METFORMIN AND IBUPROFEN

I was recently diagnosed with type 2 diabetes and my physician has prescribed Metformin 
500mg twice daily with meals. I have been taking this medication for about three weeks now 
and overall I am tolerating it reasonably well although I did experience some nausea and 
gastrointestinal discomfort during the first week which has mostly subsided.

My question concerns a potential drug interaction. I also suffer from chronic lower back 
pain and I have been managing this with over the counter ibuprofen approximately 400mg 
three times per day as needed. I have read conflicting information online about whether 
it is safe to combine Metformin with NSAIDs like ibuprofen on a regular basis.

Some sources suggest that ibuprofen and other NSAIDs can affect kidney function and that 
this could potentially interfere with Metformin clearance from the body. There is also 
some concern about lactic acidosis risk when kidney function is impaired in patients 
taking Metformin. I do not have any known kidney problems but I am now worried about 
whether regular NSAID use could create a problem over time.

Additionally I have heard that ibuprofen may have some effect on blood glucose levels. 
Is there any evidence that regular NSAID use could interfere with glycemic control in 
diabetic patients taking Metformin?

I should mention that I am 52 years old, moderately overweight at a BMI of 28, and I 
do not have any other significant medical conditions. My last HbA1c reading was 7.4 percent 
and my kidney function tests from three months ago were all within normal range.

I am planning to discuss this with my doctor at my next appointment in six weeks but I 
wanted to understand the issue better beforehand. Should I consider switching to 
acetaminophen for pain management in the meantime?
